# Competitor Landscape

Last reviewed: March 2026

---

## Category 1: Direct Competitors
*No-code AI receptionists targeting SMBs and service businesses — same buyer, same use case.*

### My AI Front Desk — myaifrontdesk.com
**Positioning**: Simplest, most affordable AI receptionist for solopreneurs and small teams.
**Channels**: Voice, SMS, web chat, web voicebot
**Strengths**: 8,500+ verified reviews (5.0 rating), $99/month entry price, keeps existing phone number, 7,000+ integrations via API
**Weaknesses**: No email, WhatsApp, or LINE support. Targets solopreneurs — lighter on enterprise credibility.
**Threat level**: High. Most direct overlap with Solvea's positioning and buyer.

---

## Category 2: Voice-First AI Platforms
*Strong in phone/voice automation but limited or absent on digital channels.*

### Bland AI — bland.ai
**Positioning**: Enterprise voice AI infrastructure with complete data sovereignty.
**Channels**: Voice (phone) only
**Strengths**: Owns entire stack (no third-party APIs), SOC 2, strong enterprise trust, 65%+ FCR
**Weaknesses**: Developer-heavy, no no-code builder, voice only, enterprise pricing
**Threat level**: Low for SMB; medium for enterprise expansion.

### Synthflow — synthflow.ai
**Positioning**: No-code voice AI with enterprise-grade reliability.
**Channels**: Voice (primary), WhatsApp, SMS follow-ups, chat
**Strengths**: Owned telephony infrastructure, SOC 2 + HIPAA + PCI + GDPR, sub-100ms latency, no-code builder
**Weaknesses**: Voice-first — digital channels are secondary, not native
**Threat level**: Medium. Overlaps on no-code and compliance but voice-centric.

---

## Category 3: AI Helpdesk & Chat Platforms
*Strong on text/digital channels, weak or absent on voice.*

### Intercom — intercom.com
**Positioning**: #1 AI agent and helpdesk for customer service (enterprise).
**Channels**: Web chat, email, help center; limited voice
**Strengths**: 30,000+ customers, top-ranked on G2 (97 categories), Fin AI Agent (66% resolution rate), massive integration ecosystem
**Weaknesses**: Expensive, complex, not built for voice or appointment booking, overkill for SMBs
**Threat level**: Low for SMB/service market; high if Solvea moves upmarket.

### Tidio — tidio.com
**Positioning**: SMB-friendly AI + human chat platform, e-commerce focused.
**Channels**: Live chat, email ticketing, social platforms; no voice
**Strengths**: 300,000+ businesses, free plan, Lyro AI (67% resolution rate), strong e-commerce integrations
**Weaknesses**: No voice support, chat/text only, less suited for appointment-heavy service businesses
**Threat level**: Medium for e-commerce SMBs; low for service-business buyers.

---

## Category 4: Hybrid AI + Human Receptionists
*Blend AI automation with live human agents as fallback — different cost and trust model.*

### Smith.ai — smith.ai
**Positioning**: AI + 500+ live North American agents for businesses that can't risk a missed call.
**Channels**: Voice, SMS; limited digital
**Strengths**: Human fallback is a real trust differentiator, 7,000+ integrations, strong in legal and home services
**Weaknesses**: Starts at $300/month for human tier, not scalable like pure AI, limited digital channels
**Threat level**: Low on price; medium for buyers who want a human safety net.
